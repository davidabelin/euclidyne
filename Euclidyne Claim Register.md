# Euclidyne Claim Register

This note records how `Euclidorithm QA.md` was filtered into the first Euclidyne build.

## Verified

- The Euclidean algorithm on positive integers yields the finite continued fraction of `a / b`.
- Extended Euclid produces coefficients `x` and `y` with `a*x + b*y = gcd(a, b)`.
- If `gcd(a, b) = 1`, the `x` coefficient gives the inverse of `a mod b`.
- Rectangle-to-squares dissection follows the same quotient sequence as the Euclidean algorithm.
- The logarithmic-spiral story belongs to the special golden-rectangle case, not to every Euclidean rectangle dissection.

## Analogy Only

- One Euclidean division step can be visualized as `q` full turns plus residual phase `r / b`.
- The Phase Lab is a one-step cycle analogy. It is not presented as a complete mechanical Euclid machine.

## Deferred

- Automatic compound-gear or sliding-gear Euclid machines.
- Claims that a sliding center shift equals the integer remainder exactly.
- Generic inward-spiral or logarithmic-spiral claims for gear or rectangle models.
- Historical claims that compound gear trains themselves implemented Euclid as an exact mechanical algorithm.
